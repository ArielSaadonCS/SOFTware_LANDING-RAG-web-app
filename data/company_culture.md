# Company Culture — TechCorp

## Overview

Understanding TechCorp's culture, history, and values is just as important as mastering the technical tools. This document gives you the full picture of who we are, what we believe, and how we work together.

---

## Company History

### Founding

TechCorp was founded in **2011** by **Sarah Chen** and **Marcus Webb** in a small co-working space in Austin, Texas. The two met while working at a legacy enterprise software firm and shared a common frustration: the tools businesses relied on were slow, opaque, and built for the needs of IT departments rather than the people actually doing the work.

Their founding hypothesis was simple — productivity software should be invisible. It should get out of the way and let people focus on what they do best.

### Early Years (2011–2015)

The company's first product, **FlowBoard**, was a project management tool designed for small engineering teams. It gained traction quickly in the startup ecosystem, reaching **10,000 active users** within 18 months without any paid marketing. The team grew to 25 employees and closed a **$4.2M Series A** round in early 2013.

By 2015, FlowBoard had evolved into a broader operational platform. TechCorp moved its headquarters to **San Francisco** and expanded internationally with an office in **London**.

### Growth and Maturity (2016–2022)

A pivotal moment came in 2016 when TechCorp launched its API platform, allowing third-party developers to build integrations on top of TechCorp infrastructure. The developer ecosystem grew to over **800 published integrations** by 2020.

In 2019, TechCorp raised a **$75M Series C** and expanded its product to include AI-assisted workflows. The pandemic years accelerated remote work adoption globally, and TechCorp's revenue grew **3x** between 2020 and 2022 as organizations sought better tools for distributed teams.

### Today

TechCorp now employs over **1,200 people** across offices in San Francisco, London, Singapore, and Warsaw. The product serves **15,000+ businesses** in 60 countries. The company remains privately held and is committed to building sustainable, long-term value.

## Core Values

TechCorp's culture is grounded in five core values. These are not posters on a wall — they are the lens through which we make decisions, hire, and evaluate performance.

### 1. Clarity Over Cleverness

We value clear communication and simple solutions over complex ones. When writing code, documentation, or an email, ask yourself: will someone unfamiliar with this context understand it immediately? If not, simplify it. Ambiguity is expensive.

### 2. Ownership Without Ego

Every employee at TechCorp is expected to own their work end-to-end — from scoping to shipping to monitoring in production. This means raising your hand when something is wrong, even if you didn't cause the problem. It also means celebrating the team's wins before your own.

### 3. Default to Openness

Information should flow freely inside TechCorp. We use public Slack channels over DMs, open-source our internal tooling where possible, and document decisions in writing so anyone can understand the reasoning behind them — months or years later.

### 4. Relentless Iteration

We ship early, learn fast, and improve continuously. Perfection is the enemy of progress. We celebrate shipping an imperfect v1 that solves a real user problem over spending months polishing something nobody has validated yet.

### 5. Human-Centered Work

We believe that building great products requires healthy, rested, and fulfilled people. We do not glamorize overwork. We protect weekends and evenings, encourage taking all allotted vacation, and measure output by impact — not hours logged.

## Routine Events

### All-Hands Meeting

**Frequency**: Monthly, on the first Thursday of each month
**Time**: 10:00 AM – 11:30 AM Pacific (recorded and posted to the internal wiki by EOD Friday)
**Format**: Virtual-first (Zoom); SF and London offices have optional in-person viewing rooms

The All-Hands is the single most important recurring event at TechCorp. It includes:

- **Company metrics update**: Key business metrics reviewed by the CEO.
- **Product spotlight**: A demo of something shipped in the past month.
- **Team shoutouts**: Recognition of standout contributions nominated by peers.
- **Open Q&A**: Any employee can ask any question, anonymously if preferred, via the Slido integration.

Attendance is strongly encouraged for all employees. The recording is always available if a conflict arises.

### Engineering Sync

**Frequency**: Every other Tuesday, 9:00 AM – 10:00 AM Pacific
**Format**: Zoom

A cross-team engineering forum where teams share progress, technical debt discussions surface, and cross-team dependencies are identified early. New engineers are expected to introduce themselves in their first Engineering Sync after joining.

### Friday Demos

**Frequency**: Weekly, every Friday at 3:00 PM Pacific
**Format**: Async-first (Loom video) with live watch party optional

Any employee can post a 5-minute Loom demo of something they built, learned, or improved that week. This is entirely voluntary but highly valued culturally. It keeps the company connected to what's being built in real time and builds a culture of shipping.

### Quarterly Planning (QBR)

**Frequency**: Once per quarter (first two weeks of January, April, July, and October)
**Format**: Mix of team workshops and company-wide briefings

Each department presents its OKRs for the upcoming quarter, reviews results from the prior quarter, and aligns with cross-functional dependencies. Individual contributors participate in team-level planning workshops. Company-wide OKRs are published on the internal wiki by the end of the first month of each quarter.

### Annual Company Offsite

**Frequency**: Once per year, typically in September
**Duration**: 3 days

The entire company gathers in person for the annual offsite. This is TechCorp's most important cultural event of the year. It combines strategic planning sessions, workshops, team activities, and social time. Attendance is mandatory and all travel and accommodation costs are covered by the company.
