### 1.2 United States — At-Will Employment & Leave Policy

#### 1.2.1 Employment Status

Employees in the United States are employed **at-will**, meaning either TechCorp or the employee may terminate the employment relationship at any time, for any reason not prohibited by law, with or without cause, and with or without notice — except where a written employment agreement, collective bargaining agreement, or specific state statute provides otherwise.

At-will status does not, however, limit TechCorp's obligations under the following statutes, each of which governs leave entitlements regardless of at-will status:

- **FMLA** (Family and Medical Leave Act, 29 U.S.C. § 2601): Up to **12 weeks** of unpaid, job-protected leave per 12-month period for qualifying medical or family reasons. Employees must have worked at TechCorp for at least 12 months and 1,250 hours to be eligible.
- **ADA** (Americans with Disabilities Act, 42 U.S.C. § 12101): TechCorp is required to provide reasonable accommodations, which may include modified leave schedules, for employees with qualifying disabilities.
- **USERRA** (Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act): Employees called to military service are entitled to reemployment rights per federal statute.

#### 1.2.2 Vacation Leave — United States

- **Accrual**: 20 days per calendar year, accruing at 1.67 days per month starting from date of hire.
- **Cap**: Accrued vacation is capped at **30 days**. No additional days accrue once the cap is reached. This cap complies with applicable state wage and hour laws; in states where vacation is treated as earned wages (California, Illinois, Colorado, Minnesota), accrued days are not forfeited and no "use-it-or-lose-it" policy applies.
- **Rollover**: Up to **5 days** roll over on January 1st, except in states where forfeiture is prohibited. In California, Colorado, Illinois, and Minnesota, all accrued and unused vacation carries forward indefinitely, subject only to the 30-day accrual cap.
- **Payout on Separation**: In all states where accrued vacation constitutes wages (California, Colorado, Illinois, Montana, Nebraska), unused accrued vacation will be paid out in the final paycheck. In states without such requirements, unused days above the rollover cap are forfeited. Employees are strongly encouraged to use accrued vacation prior to separation.

#### 1.2.3 Sick Leave — United States

TechCorp provides **10 days of paid sick leave** per year. In addition, employees in the following jurisdictions accrue state-mandated paid sick leave. Where state or city minimums exceed TechCorp's 10-day global baseline, employees receive whichever is greater — TechCorp's policy is not additive to statutory minimums, but always meets or exceeds them.

| State / City | Mandatory Accrual | TechCorp Provides |
|---|---|---|
| California | 3 days / 40 hours (SB 616: 40 hours) | 10 days (exceeds mandate) |
| New York City | 56 hours (employers 100+) | 10 days (exceeds mandate) |
| Washington State | 1 hour per 40 hours worked | 10 days (exceeds mandate) |
| Colorado (HFWA) | 48 hours (6 days) | 10 days (exceeds mandate) |
| All other US states | Per applicable law | 10 days (meets or exceeds) |

Sick leave may be used for: employee illness or injury; care of a family member; preventive care appointments; mental health; domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking-related needs as defined by applicable state law.

Documentation (a medical provider's note) may be requested if absence exceeds **3 consecutive business days**. TechCorp will not require documentation in a manner that violates applicable state law (e.g., California law restricts documentation requests in certain contexts).

#### 1.2.4 FMLA Administration — United States

Employees wishing to invoke FMLA must:

1. Notify HR (`fmla@techcorp.io`) within **2 business days** of learning about the qualifying need, or as soon as practicable in emergencies.
2. Complete the **FMLA Eligibility and Notice Form** (DOL Form WH-381), provided by HR within 5 business days of notification.
3. Submit the appropriate medical certification form (DOL Form WH-380-E for employee, WH-380-F for family member) within **15 calendar days** of request.

FMLA leave may run concurrently with accrued paid leave. TechCorp requires employees to exhaust accrued sick leave and vacation leave concurrently with FMLA leave. FMLA leave is job-protected; the employee will return to the same or an equivalent position.

### 1.3 European Union — Statutory Leave & GDPR Compliance

#### 1.3.1 Jurisdictional Scope

This section applies to employees employed by TechCorp entities in EU Member States and the European Economic Area (EEA), including: **Germany, France, the Netherlands, Ireland, Sweden, Denmark, Spain, and Poland**. Employees in the United Kingdom are governed by the UK Addendum, which mirrors EU standards post-Brexit with minor variations.

#### 1.3.2 Vacation Leave — EU

The EU Working Time Directive (Directive 2003/88/EC) establishes a **minimum of 4 weeks (20 working days)** of paid annual leave. TechCorp's global baseline of 20 days satisfies this minimum in all EU jurisdictions. However, statutory minimums in several member states exceed the Directive:

| Country | Statutory Minimum | TechCorp Provides |
|---|---|---|
| Austria | 25 days (after 25 years: 30) | 25 days baseline |
| France | 25 days (Congés payés) + 11 public holidays | 25 days |
| Germany | 20 days (federal minimum; 24–30 days typical) | 28 days |
| Netherlands | 20 days (4x weekly hours) | 25 days |
| Sweden | 25 days (Semester Act, 1977) | 25 days |
| Denmark | 25 days | 25 days |
| Poland | 20 days (<10 years service); 26 days (10+ years) | 26 days |
| Ireland | 20 days | 22 days |
| Spain | 22 working days | 22 days |

**Carryover**: EU law permits member states to restrict carryover periods. At TechCorp EU entities, unused vacation may be carried over for a maximum of **18 months** from the end of the leave year, after which it expires. Employees on long-term sick leave or parental leave are entitled to carry over leave for at least 18 months per the Schultz-Hoff jurisprudence of the CJEU.

**Payout on Termination**: Across all EU jurisdictions, accrued and unused vacation leave constitutes a statutory right and must be paid out upon termination of employment, without exception.

#### 1.3.3 Sick Leave — EU

Statutory sick pay obligations vary significantly across member states. TechCorp harmonizes across these obligations as follows:

| Country | Statutory Sick Pay | TechCorp Policy |
|---|---|---|
| Germany | Employer pays 100% for 6 weeks (Entgeltfortzahlung); then statutory health insurance | 10 days full pay, then statutory entitlement applies |
| France | Employer pays from Day 4 (3-day waiting period); CPAM supplementation | TechCorp waives the waiting period; 10 days full pay |
| Netherlands | Employer pays 70% of salary for up to 2 years (Wet Verbetering Poortwachter) | 100% for 30 days; 70% statutory rate thereafter |
| Sweden | Day 1: qualifying deduction (karensdag); Day 2–14: employer pays 80%; Day 15+: Försäkringskassan | TechCorp waives karensdag; 10 days full pay |
| Ireland | No statutory sick pay above SSP floor (Sick Leave Act 2022: 5 days in 2025) | 10 days full pay (exceeds mandate) |
| Poland | Days 1–33: employer pays 80%; Day 34+: ZUS pays | 10 days full pay |
| Spain | Days 1–3: no pay; Days 4–15: 60% (employer); Day 16+: 75% (INSS) | TechCorp covers Days 1–3; 10 days full pay |

For absences exceeding 3 consecutive days, a medical certificate (*Arbeitsunfähigkeitsbescheinigung* in Germany, *arrêt de travail* in France, etc.) is required in the format prescribed by national law. HR will notify employees of country-specific documentation requirements.

#### 1.3.4 Employee Data & GDPR Obligations

TechCorp processes employee personal data as a data controller under **Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (General Data Protection Regulation)**. The following governs all HR-related data processing:

**Legal Bases for Processing**: TechCorp relies on the following legal bases for processing employee data:

| Data Category | Legal Basis (GDPR Art.) | Retention Period |
|---|---|---|
| Employment contract and identity | Art. 6(1)(b) — contractual necessity | Duration of employment + 7 years |
| Payroll and tax records | Art. 6(1)(c) — legal obligation | 10 years (tax law) |
| Performance reviews | Art. 6(1)(b) — contractual necessity | Duration of employment + 3 years |
| Health / sick leave data | Art. 9(2)(b) — employment law obligation | 5 years (subject to local law) |
| Disciplinary records | Art. 6(1)(f) — legitimate interests | Duration of employment + 3 years |
| Background check results | Art. 6(1)(c) / Art. 9(2)(b) | 6 months post-hire (or as legally required) |
| CCTV footage (office premises) | Art. 6(1)(f) — legitimate interests | 30 days maximum |

**Employee Rights (GDPR Chapter III)**: EU employees have the right to:

- **Access (Art. 15)**: Request a copy of all personal data held about them. Submit requests to `privacy@techcorp.io`. Response within 30 days.
- **Rectification (Art. 16)**: Correct inaccurate personal data.
- **Erasure (Art. 17)**: Request deletion of data where no legal retention obligation applies. Note: employment records cannot be erased during the statutory retention period.
- **Restriction (Art. 18)**: Restrict processing in defined circumstances.
- **Portability (Art. 20)**: Receive personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.
- **Object (Art. 21)**: Object to processing based on legitimate interests.

**Cross-Border Data Transfers**: TechCorp transfers EU employee data to its US headquarters and other international entities. Transfers rely on **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** approved by the European Commission under Decision 2021/914/EU. An up-to-date Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) is maintained by the Data Protection Officer and available to employees upon request.

**DPO Contact**: TechCorp's Data Protection Officer for EU processing activities is reachable at `dpo@techcorp.io`.

### 1.4 APAC Region — Leave Policy

#### 1.4.1 Jurisdictional Scope

This section applies to employees employed by TechCorp APAC entities in: **Singapore, Australia, Japan, India, and South Korea**.

#### 1.4.2 Singapore

Leave entitlements are governed by the **Employment Act (Cap. 91A)** and the **Child Development Co-Savings Act**:

- **Annual Leave**: Statutory minimum scales from 7 days (Year 1) to 14 days (Year 8+). TechCorp provides a flat **18 days** from the first day of employment.
- **Sick Leave**: 14 days outpatient; 60 days hospitalization (inclusive of outpatient entitlement) per the Employment Act. TechCorp provides this in full.
- **Childcare Leave**: 6 days per year per parent for children under 7 (Singapore citizen child). 2 days for permanent resident children.
- **Paternity Leave**: 2 weeks (Government-Paid Paternity Leave, GPPL) for Singapore citizen children born on or after 1 January 2024.
- **Maternity Leave**: 16 weeks for Singapore citizen child; 12 weeks for non-citizen child (Employment Act S. 76).
- **NS Leave**: Employees mobilized under the National Service (Enlistment) Act receive paid leave for the duration of in-camp training, with no impact on annual leave balance.

#### 1.4.3 Australia

Leave entitlements are governed by the **Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)** and the **National Employment Standards (NES)**:

- **Annual Leave**: 4 weeks (20 days) per year. Shift workers receive 5 weeks. Leave accrues continuously.
- **Annual Leave Loading**: A leave loading of **17.5%** of the ordinary rate of pay applies to annual leave taken, consistent with many Modern Awards and the prevailing TechCorp Enterprise Agreement.
- **Personal / Carer's Leave**: 10 days per year (paid). Employees may use this for their own illness or to care for a family or household member. Unused personal leave rolls over year-to-year without a cap.
- **Compassionate Leave**: 2 days per occasion (paid) for death or serious illness of an immediate family or household member.
- **Parental Leave**: Up to 12 months of unpaid parental leave with the right to request an additional 12 months. Government-funded paid parental leave (PPL) via Services Australia runs in parallel; TechCorp tops up to full pay for the first **16 weeks**.
- **Public Holidays**: Governed by applicable state/territory laws. Employees required to work on a public holiday are entitled to a substitute day off or penalty rates as required by the Fair Work Act.
- **Long Service Leave**: Governed by state law. In New South Wales (LSL Act 1955), employees become entitled to 2 months of long service leave after 10 years of service. TechCorp tracks and manages LSL entitlements centrally via the HR portal.

#### 1.4.4 Japan

Leave entitlements are governed by the **Labor Standards Act (労働基準法, LSA)**:

- **Annual Leave (有給休暇)**: The LSA mandates annual paid leave scaling from **10 days** (after 6 months of continuous service and 80% attendance) to **20 days** (after 6.5+ years). TechCorp provides a flat **20 days** from commencement to avoid complexity.
- **Mandatory Leave Consumption**: Under 2019 LSA amendments, employees must take a minimum of **5 days** of annual leave per year. Managers are legally responsible for ensuring their team members satisfy this requirement. Failure to comply may result in fines up to ¥300,000 per employee per year under LSA Art. 39.
- **Sick Leave (病気休暇)**: Japan has no statutory paid sick leave requirement. TechCorp provides **10 days paid sick leave** to align with the global baseline. A medical certificate is required from Day 1 for foreseeable absences; from Day 3 for unforeseeable illness.
- **Maternity Leave (産前産後休業)**: 6 weeks before and 8 weeks after childbirth (LSA Art. 65). The post-birth period is mandatory; the pre-birth period is employee-elected.
- **Childcare Leave (育児休業)**: Under the Childcare and Family Care Leave Act (育児・介護休業法), both parents may take childcare leave until the child reaches age 2 in certain circumstances. From April 2022, new obligations require employers to notify eligible employees of their rights.

#### 1.4.5 India

Leave entitlements vary by state under the **Shops and Establishments Acts** and the **Factories Act, 1948**. The following represents TechCorp India's harmonized policy, which meets or exceeds statutory requirements in all states of operation (Karnataka, Maharashtra, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Delhi NCR):

- **Earned Leave (EL)**: **18 days** per year, accruing at 1.5 days per month. A maximum of **30 EL days** may be carried forward at year-end; surplus days are encashed at the end of Q1 of the following year at the employee's basic salary rate.
- **Casual Leave (CL)**: **12 days** per year. Non-rollover; CL balance expires on December 31st. Minimum half-day increments.
- **Sick Leave (SL)**: **12 days** per year. A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 2 consecutive days.
- **Maternity Leave**: **26 weeks** of paid maternity leave for the first two children, per the **Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017**. 12 weeks for the third child onward.
- **Paternity Leave**: TechCorp India provides **5 days** of paid paternity leave (no statutory requirement exists; this is a discretionary TechCorp benefit).
- **Public Holidays**: 12 national and state-designated holidays as published per the annual calendar. Three optional holidays from a regional list are available for personal or religious observance.

6. Reports containing any single item over **$500** automatically route to the Department Head for **Tier 2 approval**.
7. Reports containing any single item over **$2,000** additionally route to the VP of Finance for **Tier 3 approval**.
8. Finance processes fully approved reports within **7–10 business days** via the employee's designated bank account.

### 3.2 Requesting Non-Standard Hardware

Non-standard hardware includes: additional monitors, ergonomic peripherals (standing desk converters, specialized chairs, wrist supports), upgraded laptops, tablets, or any hardware not in the standard allocation table above.

#### 3.2.1 Tier 1 Request — Items Under $300

For ergonomic accessories and peripherals under $300:

1. Submit a **Hardware Request** ticket in ServiceNow (`servicenow.techcorp.io > HR Services > Hardware Request`).
2. Select **Tier 1 — Standard Peripheral** and provide a brief business justification (1–2 sentences).
3. Your direct manager receives an automated approval notification and has **3 business days** to approve or deny.
4. Upon manager approval, IT Procurement processes the order. Estimated delivery: 5–10 business days (domestic), 10–20 business days (international).

#### 3.2.2 Tier 2 Request — Items $300–$1,500

For upgraded monitors, docking stations, and other equipment in this range:

1. Submit a **Hardware Request** ticket in ServiceNow (`Tier 2 — Equipment Upgrade`).
2. Provide a detailed business justification explaining why the standard allocation is insufficient for your role. Justifications must reference specific, measurable productivity or health impacts.
3. Your direct manager reviews and, if approved, escalates to your **Department Head** for secondary approval.
4. If both approvals are obtained, the request routes to IT Procurement for budgetary confirmation against the department's hardware budget.
5. Total approval-to-order time: 5–8 business days. Requests denied at any stage are returned to the submitter with written reasoning within 2 business days.

#### 3.2.3 Tier 3 Request — Items Over $1,500 or Specialized Hardware

For non-standard laptops, server hardware, specialized A/V equipment, or development boards:

1. Submit a **Hardware Request** ticket in ServiceNow (`Tier 3 — Specialized Equipment`) including:
   - Item description, vendor, model number, and quoted price (attach vendor quote)
   - Business justification (must reference a specific project or deliverable)
   - Alternative considered and reason it was rejected
   - Expected useful life
2. Approval chain: Direct Manager → Department Head → VP of Engineering (or equivalent function head) → IT Procurement.
3. Each approver has **3 business days** to respond. Unresponsive approvers will be escalated by IT Procurement after the window lapses.
4. IT Procurement may substitute an equivalent item from a preferred vendor at lower cost. Substitution proposals are shared with the requestor before ordering.
5. Total time to order: 10–15 business days. Items over $5,000 require Finance Director co-approval and may be subject to capitalization as a fixed asset.

#### 3.2.4 Laptop Refresh Cycle

Standard laptop refresh occurs on a **3-year cycle** from the date of issue. Refresh requests submitted before 3 years require documented performance degradation evidence (crash logs, diagnostic reports) and manager approval. Laptops returned after refresh are wiped and redeployed to new joiners or sold via the company's certified hardware recycling partner.

## Section 4 — Disciplinary Action Protocol

### 4.1 Guiding Principles

TechCorp's disciplinary process is designed to be **corrective, not punitive** wherever possible. The goal is to give employees a clear, documented opportunity to meet performance or conduct expectations before escalation occurs. This process applies to performance deficiencies and behavioral violations alike, except where immediate termination is warranted under Section 4.5.

All disciplinary actions are documented, stored in the employee's confidential HR file, and retained per the data retention schedules in Section 5. Employees have the right to respond to any disciplinary documentation in writing within **5 business days** of receipt. Written responses are attached to the disciplinary record.

Managers initiating any formal disciplinary action must engage their **HR Business Partner** before taking any step beyond an informal verbal conversation. Proceeding with formal discipline without HR involvement exposes TechCorp to legal risk and may itself be treated as a management conduct issue.

### 4.2 Informal Counseling

Before initiating a formal process, managers are expected to attempt informal resolution through direct, documented conversations with the employee. Informal counseling is appropriate for first-time, minor performance gaps or low-severity conduct issues.

**Process**:

1. Manager schedules a private 1:1 meeting with the employee.
2. Manager clearly and specifically describes the observed performance gap or behavior.
3. Manager articulates the expected standard and the consequences of not meeting it.
4. Discussion is summarized in a brief internal email from manager to employee (CC: HR Business Partner), retained in the manager's records but not placed in the formal HR file unless escalation follows.
5. A follow-up check-in is scheduled no later than **30 days** out.

If the issue is resolved after informal counseling, no further action is required. If the issue persists or recurs, the formal process begins with a Written Warning.

### 4.3 Formal Written Warning

A Written Warning is issued when: informal counseling has not resolved the issue; the issue is serious enough to bypass informal counseling at the manager's and HR's discretion; or the employee has received informal counseling within the past 6 months for a related issue.

**Process**:

1. HR Business Partner drafts the Written Warning in collaboration with the manager.
2. Warning must include:
   - Specific description of the performance gap or conduct violation, with dates and examples.
   - The expected standard and measurable improvement criteria.
   - Timeline for demonstrating improvement (typically **30–60 days**).
   - Statement of consequences if improvement criteria are not met.
3. Manager and HR Business Partner meet with the employee to deliver the warning in person or via video call. A note-taker (a second HR representative or manager's manager) must be present.
4. Employee is given a copy of the warning and **5 business days** to submit a written response.
5. The warning and any employee response are placed in the employee's formal HR file.

### 4.4 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

A Performance Improvement Plan is initiated when: a Written Warning has not produced the required improvement within the specified period; a performance gap is severe enough to warrant skipping directly to PIP (with HR Director approval); or the employee's performance represents a material risk to the team or business.

**A PIP is a formal, legally significant document.** It must be reviewed and approved by the HR Director and the relevant VP before delivery. In EU jurisdictions, works council consultation requirements and notice periods must be observed before a PIP is initiated.

#### 4.4.1 PIP Content Requirements

A PIP must contain all of the following elements:

1. **Employee Information**: Full name, job title, department, manager, start date.
2. **Background**: A factual narrative of the performance deficiencies, referencing prior informal counseling and written warning documentation.
3. **Specific Performance Goals**: Each goal must be **SMART** (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). Vague goals such as "improve communication" are not acceptable without concrete measurement criteria (e.g., "complete all sprint tasks as committed in 4 of 6 upcoming sprints").
4. **Support Provided**: Documentation of what support TechCorp will provide during the PIP period: additional training, mentorship, adjusted workload, regular check-ins, etc.
5. **Check-In Schedule**: Mandatory check-in meetings, typically **bi-weekly**, documented in writing with notes on progress.
6. **Duration**: Standard PIP duration is **60–90 days**. Duration must be sufficient to allow genuine opportunity for improvement.
7. **Consequence Statement**: Explicit statement that failure to meet the PIP goals by the end of the PIP period may result in termination of employment.

#### 4.4.2 PIP Administration Process

| Step | Responsible Party | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Draft PIP document | HR Business Partner + Manager | 5 business days |
| Legal review (EU entities) | Employment Law Counsel | 5–10 business days |
| Works council notification (Germany, France, Netherlands) | HR Director | Per applicable law |
| HR Director and VP approval | HR Director + Function VP | 3 business days |
| Employee delivery meeting | Manager + HR Business Partner | Within 2 business days of approval |
| Employee acknowledgment (signature) | Employee | 3 business days post-delivery |
| Bi-weekly progress check-ins | Manager + HR Business Partner | Throughout PIP duration |
| Final assessment meeting | Manager + HR Business Partner + HR Director | Final week of PIP period |
| Outcome decision | HR Director + Function VP | Within 5 business days of final assessment |

**Possible PIP Outcomes**:

- **Successful completion**: PIP closes. Employee record reflects successful completion. A 6-month monitoring period follows; if the same performance issues recur within 6 months, PIP may be re-issued or termination recommended without a new written warning.
- **Partial improvement — PIP extension**: In exceptional cases with documented material progress, the HR Director may approve a PIP extension of up to **30 additional days**. No PIP may be extended more than once.
- **Failure to meet goals**: The HR Director, VP, and Legal counsel convene to determine whether termination is appropriate. Termination is not automatic; all facts are reviewed. Termination following a PIP is subject to the standard notice and severance obligations under applicable law (see Section 4.6).

### 4.5 Immediate Termination for Cause

The following violations may result in **immediate termination without prior warning or PIP process**, subject to applicable employment law in the relevant jurisdiction. In jurisdictions requiring cause to be established before termination (all EU member states, UK, Australia, Singapore, Japan, India, South Korea), TechCorp will document the factual basis for cause and complete any legally mandated consultation before acting.

**Grounds for immediate termination include, but are not limited to**:

- Theft, fraud, or financial dishonesty involving company funds or assets
- Falsification of employment documents, qualifications, time records, or expense reports with the intent to deceive
- Serious harassment, including sexual harassment, involving physical contact, threats, or a pattern of behavior that constitutes a hostile work environment
- Disclosure of trade secrets or proprietary information to a competitor or unauthorized party
- Conduct that causes material harm to TechCorp's reputation, clients, or operations
- Violation of applicable law in the conduct of company business
- Use, possession, or distribution of illegal substances on company premises or during company events
- Unauthorized access to or exfiltration of company, employee, or client data (including violations of the data classification policy in Section 5)
- Repeated and willful non-compliance with the information security policies in `it_setup_guide.md` after documented warnings

### 4.6 Termination — Voluntary Resignation and Company-Initiated

#### 4.6.1 Voluntary Resignation

Employees resigning from TechCorp must provide written notice per the following schedule, unless their employment agreement specifies a longer notice period:

| Tenure | Required Notice |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 year | 2 weeks |
| 1–3 years | 4 weeks |
| 3–7 years | 6 weeks |
| 7+ years | 8 weeks |

In EU jurisdictions, statutory notice minimums override the above where they are more favorable to the employee. In Germany, the minimum statutory notice period scales from 4 weeks (< 2 years) to 7 months (20+ years) under § 622 BGB.

#### 4.6.2 Company-Initiated Termination (Without Cause)

Employees terminated for reasons other than cause (e.g., role elimination, restructuring, or performance that does not meet the PIP threshold for cause) are entitled to notice and severance per the following schedule:

| Tenure | Notice Period | Severance Pay |
|---|---|---|
| < 1 year | 4 weeks | 1 month base salary |
| 1–3 years | 6 weeks | 1.5 months base salary per year |
| 3–5 years | 8 weeks | 2 months base salary per year |
| 5–10 years | 12 weeks | 2.5 months base salary per year |
| 10+ years | 16 weeks | 3 months base salary per year |

The above are **minimums**. Statutory entitlements in applicable jurisdictions supersede this schedule where they are more favorable. In Australia, the NES provides statutory redundancy pay; in the EU, individual member states have mandatory minimum severance regimes; in Singapore, the Employment Act sets minimum retrenchment benefit guidelines. TechCorp's policy is to comply with or exceed all statutory requirements.

#### 4.6.3 Separation Formalities

Upon termination (voluntary or company-initiated), the following must be completed:

1. **IT Access Revocation**: All system access is revoked on the last day of employment (or on the date of termination notice for cause). IT receives automated notification from the HR system within 1 hour of HR recording the termination.
2. **Hardware Return**: All company hardware must be returned within **5 business days** of the final day of employment. Failure to return equipment results in deduction of the asset's book value from the final paycheck, to the extent permitted by applicable law.
3. **Exit Interview**: HR will schedule a voluntary exit interview to gather feedback. Participation is optional but strongly encouraged.
4. **Final Paycheck**: Processed per the payroll schedule of the applicable jurisdiction. In California, the final paycheck (including all accrued vacation) must be issued on the final day of employment. In other US states and internationally, applicable statutory deadlines govern.
5. **COBRA / Continuation Coverage Notification (US only)**: Employees and dependents losing health coverage due to termination will be notified of their rights under COBRA within 14 days per 29 U.S.C. § 1166.
6. **References**: TechCorp's policy is to confirm dates of employment, title, and eligibility for rehire only. Managers must not provide personal references in their official capacity without prior HR approval.

## Section 5 — Data Privacy & Information Classification

### 5.1 Scope and Legal Framework

This section applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties who handle TechCorp data. Non-compliance with this policy constitutes a disciplinary offense and may result in termination. In addition to internal policy consequences, non-compliance may expose individuals to civil liability or criminal prosecution under applicable data protection laws, including:

- **GDPR** (EU/EEA, UK)
- **CCPA / CPRA** (California)
- **PDPA** (Singapore Personal Data Protection Act)
- **Privacy Act 1988** (Australia)
- **APPI** (Act on the Protection of Personal Information, Japan)
- **PDPB** (India Personal Data Protection Bill, when enacted)

### 5.2 Data Classification Framework

All data created, processed, transmitted, or stored by TechCorp must be classified into one of the following four tiers. Employees are responsible for classifying data they create. When in doubt, classify at the higher level.

#### 5.2.1 Public

**Definition**: Information that has been formally approved for public release or is already in the public domain.

**Examples**: Press releases, published blog posts, open-source code under an approved license, public API documentation, marketing materials.

**Handling Requirements**:
- No restrictions on storage location or transmission method.
- No encryption required (though TLS in transit is always recommended).
- No access controls beyond standard authentication.

#### 5.2.2 Internal

**Definition**: Information intended for use within TechCorp by all employees. Not approved for external distribution, but its disclosure would not cause significant harm.

**Examples**: Internal wikis, meeting recordings, project plans, general engineering documentation, this HR policy document.

**Handling Requirements**:
- Must not be shared externally without manager approval.
- Must be stored in company-managed systems (Google Drive, Confluence, GitHub enterprise). Personal cloud accounts (personal Dropbox, Google Drive, iCloud) are not permitted.
- Transmission via TechCorp-approved channels only (Slack, Google Workspace, corporate email).
- No special encryption requirements beyond standard TLS in transit and disk encryption at rest.

#### 5.2.3 Confidential

**Definition**: Sensitive business information whose unauthorized disclosure would cause moderate to significant harm to TechCorp, its employees, clients, or partners.

**Examples**: Financial projections, M&A due diligence materials, client contracts, employee salary data, performance review records, product roadmaps not yet publicly announced, security vulnerability reports, personally identifiable information (PII) of employees and customers.

**Handling Requirements**:
- **Access Control**: Strict need-to-know basis. Access must be explicitly granted; default access is denied. Access lists must be reviewed quarterly.
- **Storage**: Must be stored in designated confidential storage systems (e.g., the `confidential` folder structure in Google Drive with DLP policies applied, or encrypted S3 buckets with bucket policies).
- **Transmission**: Must be encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2+. Email transmission of confidential data to external parties is permitted only with manager approval and must be sent to corporate email addresses only (no personal Gmail, Yahoo, etc.).
- **Physical Handling**: Do not discuss confidential information in public spaces (cafés, airports, public transit). Use a privacy screen on laptops when working in public.
- **Printing**: Confidential documents printed physically must be retrieved from the printer immediately and shredded when no longer needed. Do not leave confidential printed materials at shared desk spaces.
- **Retention**: Retain only as long as necessary for the business purpose. Delete when no longer needed, in accordance with the data retention schedule maintained by Legal.

#### 5.2.4 Restricted

**Definition**: The most sensitive category. Unauthorized disclosure would cause severe harm to TechCorp, its clients, employees, or the public. Includes data whose processing is subject to specific legal controls.

**Examples**: Authentication credentials and private cryptographic keys, customer payment card data (PCI DSS scope), protected health information (HIPAA scope, if applicable), data subject to active litigation holds, security incident response data, executive communications regarding material non-public information (MNPI), EU special category personal data (Art. 9 GDPR: health, biometric, racial/ethnic, religious, political, sexual orientation data), source code for unreleased products, and trade secrets.

**Handling Requirements**:
- **Access Control**: Explicitly approved by the Data Owner (department head) and recorded in the access management system. Access is reviewed monthly. Privilege access requires MFA with a hardware security key.
- **Storage**: Encrypted at rest (AES-256 or equivalent) in designated restricted-data systems. Local storage on employee devices is prohibited unless the device is company-managed, fully encrypted, and enrolled in MDM.
- **Transmission**: End-to-end encrypted channels only. Restricted data must never be transmitted via email, Slack, or any messaging tool without encryption-at-rest protections. Use the company's secure file transfer service (`securefiles.techcorp.io`) for external transfer.
- **Logging**: All access to restricted data must be logged to a tamper-evident audit log. Logs are reviewed by the Security team weekly.
- **Prohibition on Local Copy**: Restricted data must not be downloaded to local machines without a documented, time-limited justification approved by the CISO.
- **Third-Party Sharing**: Sharing restricted data with any external party requires a signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) or Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) on file, reviewed by Legal, before any transfer occurs.

### 5.3 Handling Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

PII is any information that can be used to identify an individual, directly or indirectly. At TechCorp, PII is always classified at **Confidential or Restricted** depending on the specific data elements involved.

#### 5.3.1 PII Categories

| Category | Examples | Classification |
|---|---|---|
| Direct identifiers | Full name, email, phone, home address, national ID number | Confidential |
| Financial identifiers | Bank account numbers, credit card numbers, payroll data | Restricted |
| Health and medical data | Sick leave records, disability accommodations, insurance claims | Restricted |
| Biometric data | Fingerprints, facial recognition data, voice prints | Restricted |
| Special category (GDPR Art. 9) | Racial/ethnic origin, religious beliefs, political opinions, sexual orientation, criminal convictions | Restricted |
| Online identifiers | IP addresses (when linked to individual), cookies, device IDs | Confidential |
| Professional data | Job title, department, work email (internal directory) | Internal |

#### 5.3.2 PII Handling Rules

- **Collection Limitation**: Only collect the minimum PII necessary for the specific business purpose. Do not collect PII speculatively or "in case it's useful later."
- **Purpose Limitation**: PII collected for one purpose must not be reused for a different, incompatible purpose without obtaining a new legal basis.
- **Storage**: PII must not be stored in informal locations: Slack messages, Google Sheets accessible to the whole company, Notion pages, or meeting notes shared broadly. Use designated systems (HRIS for employee data, CRM for customer data).
- **Anonymization vs. Pseudonymization**: For analytics and testing workloads, PII must be anonymized (irreversible) or pseudonymized (reversible with a separately stored key) before use. Production PII must never be used in development or staging environments.
- **Data Subject Requests**: If an employee, customer, or any individual submits a request to access, correct, or delete their personal data, forward it immediately to `privacy@techcorp.io`. DSRs must be responded to within **30 days** (GDPR), **45 days** (CCPA), or the applicable local timeframe. Do not attempt to respond to DSRs independently.

#### 5.3.3 Data Breach Response Protocol

A data breach is any confirmed or suspected unauthorized access to, disclosure of, or destruction of personal data or confidential information. If you suspect a breach:

1. **Do not attempt to investigate or remediate independently.** Containment actions taken without Security team coordination can destroy forensic evidence.
2. **Immediately contact** `security@techcorp.io` or the 24/7 security incident hotline: `+1-833-555-0172`.
3. **Preserve evidence**: Do not delete files, logs, or messages related to the suspected breach.
4. **Do not notify affected individuals or external parties** unless instructed by Legal and the Security team.

GDPR requires notification to the relevant supervisory authority within **72 hours** of becoming aware of a breach (Art. 33). TechCorp's DPO manages this notification; individual employees are never responsible for regulatory notification.

### 5.4 Data Retention and Destruction

TechCorp maintains a global **Data Retention Schedule** published on the internal wiki (`wiki.techcorp.io/legal/data-retention`). Key retention periods:

| Data Category | Retention Period | Responsible Team |
|---|---|---|
| Employee HR records | Employment + 7 years | People Ops |
| Payroll records | 7–10 years (jurisdiction-dependent) | Finance |
| Customer transaction data | 5 years | Finance / Legal |
| Security audit logs | 2 years | Security |
| Sales contracts | Contract term + 7 years | Legal |
| Product usage telemetry (anonymized) | 3 years | Data Engineering |
| Recruitment / application data (unsuccessful) | 6 months post-rejection | Recruiting |
| Video conference recordings | 90 days (auto-deleted by Zoom policy) | IT |

When data reaches the end of its retention period, it must be **securely destroyed**:

- **Digital data**: Secure deletion via certified overwrite tool or cryptographic erasure (for encrypted storage). Certificates of destruction are issued by IT for Restricted data destruction.
- **Physical media** (hard drives, USB drives, printed documents): Destroyed by the company's certified data destruction vendor. Vendor must hold an ISO 27001 certification and provide a chain-of-custody destruction certificate.

## Section 6 — Code of Conduct

### 6.1 Our Commitment

TechCorp is committed to maintaining a workplace that is safe, respectful, inclusive, and free from discrimination and harassment. This Code of Conduct is a binding condition of employment and applies globally in all work settings: in-office, remote, business travel, company events, and all professional communications including Slack, email, and social media when acting in a professional capacity representing TechCorp.

### 6.2 Equal Employment Opportunity

TechCorp is an equal opportunity employer. Employment decisions — including hiring, promotion, compensation, performance evaluation, training, and termination — are made without regard to race, color, national origin, ancestry, religion or creed, sex, gender identity, gender expression, pregnancy, childbirth or related medical conditions, marital or domestic partner status, age, disability, medical condition, genetic information, military or veteran status, sexual orientation, or any other characteristic protected under applicable federal, state, or local law.

Managers and HR personnel who make employment decisions bearing on any of the above protected characteristics outside of legitimate, documented business criteria will be subject to immediate disciplinary action.

### 6.3 Anti-Harassment Policy

**Harassment** is defined as any unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment, or that unreasonably interferes with an employee's work performance.

**Sexual harassment** specifically includes:

- Quid pro quo harassment: conditioning employment benefits on submission to unwelcome sexual conduct.
- Hostile work environment harassment: unwelcome verbal, physical, or visual conduct of a sexual nature that is severe or pervasive.

Neither unwelcome conduct needs to be overtly sexual to constitute harassment. Comments about appearance, repeated unwanted social invitations, derogatory jokes, and persistent personal questions can all constitute harassment depending on context, frequency, and impact.

### 6.4 Reporting Violations

Employees who experience or witness a potential Code of Conduct violation must report it through one of the following channels:

| Channel | Contact | Confidentiality |
|---|---|---|
| Direct manager | Manager's email or Slack | Not anonymous |
| HR Business Partner | `hr@techcorp.io` | Confidential (not anonymous) |
| Anonymous HR Portal | `portal.techcorp.io/report` | Fully anonymous |
| Ethics Hotline (24/7) | 1-800-555-0199 | Fully anonymous |
| Legal Counsel | `legal@techcorp.io` | Privileged |

**Anti-retaliation**: Any retaliation against an employee who reports a concern in good faith — including demotion, pay reduction, schedule changes, increased scrutiny, social ostracism, or any other adverse action — is itself a serious violation of this policy and will be treated as a separate disciplinary matter. Retaliation may result in immediate termination.

### 6.5 Investigation Process

All formal reports are assigned to an **HR Investigator** (or an independent external investigator for reports involving senior leadership) within **2 business days** of receipt. The investigation process:

1. Initial assessment to determine scope and whether interim protective measures are warranted (e.g., separating the involved parties).
2. Interviews with the complainant, the respondent, and relevant witnesses. All participants are instructed to maintain confidentiality during the investigation.
3. Review of relevant documentation (emails, Slack messages, calendar records, expense records).
4. Findings report prepared and reviewed by HR Director and Legal Counsel.
5. Outcome communicated to the complainant and respondent separately and confidentially.
6. Remediation action, if warranted, implemented per Section 4.
7. Follow-up check-in with the complainant 30 days after outcome to confirm no retaliation has occurred.

Investigations are expected to conclude within **30 calendar days** of the report. Complex cases may extend to 60 days with notification to the parties.
